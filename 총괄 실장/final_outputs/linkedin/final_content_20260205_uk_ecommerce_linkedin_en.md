## Final Content

The UK is Europe's largest eCommerce market.

£286 billion. Third globally behind China and the US.

But here's what makes it different:

27% of all retail happens online.

The European average? Just 15%.

Here's what's reshaping the market in 2025:

**The Numbers:**

→ Market size: £286B ($285.6B)
→ Online shoppers: 62.1 million (90%+ of internet users)
→ Mobile transactions: 70% of all sales
→ Mobile revenue: £100B+

**The Power Players:**

Amazon: 30%+ market share. Untouchable.
Tesco: Just launched a third-party marketplace (April 2025).
Temu & Shein: Fastest-growing apps. Disrupting everything.

**3 trends you can't ignore:**

1. Mobile-first is an understatement
   → 78% of eCommerce traffic from smartphones
   → 48% of cross-border orders placed on mobile
   → Same-day delivery now in 155+ Amazon locations

2. Payment revolution
   → Digital wallets: 48% of businesses accept them
   → BNPL (Klarna, Clearpay) is mainstream
   → Open banking: 13.3M users (↑40% YoY)

3. Social commerce explosion
   → TikTok Shop and Instagram driving direct sales
   → Projected to hit £104B by 2030
   → Cross-border sales up 4% YoY

Post-Brexit reality check:

Domestic growth is slowing (3.6% in 2025).

But cross-border is accelerating.

The UK remains the gateway to European consumers—just with more paperwork.

If you're building a Europe strategy, the UK is still the market to crack first.

What's your UK play?
